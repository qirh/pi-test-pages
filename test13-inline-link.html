The U.S. State Department has suspended the processing of immigration visa applications for 75 countries, including Brazil. The information was published by Fox News Digital and later confirmed in a post by press secretary, Karoline Leavitt, on X. "The U.S. freezes all visa processing for 75 countries, including Somalia, Russia, and Iran," press secretary Karoline Leavitt posted on Fox News, with a link to a Fox News article stating that the measure referred to immigrant visas. According to the broadcaster, the suspension will begin next Wednesday, the 21st, and will continue indefinitely. The goal is to curb applicants considered likely to become a public expense. A State Department memo, which Fox News had access to, instructs consular officials to refuse visas in accordance with current legislation while the department re-evaluates screening and verification procedures. The broadcaster did not release the complete list of affected countries, but stated that, in addition to Brazil, Somalia, Russia, Afghanistan, Iran, Iraq, Egypt, Nigeria, Thailand, and Yemen are also on the list.
